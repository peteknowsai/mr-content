# Dining — Crystal Coast

Write waterfront dining and dock-and-dine cards for Crystal Coast captains.

## Morehead City Waterfront
- **Sanitary Fish Market** — Historic seafood institution (1938). Waterfront location, fresh local catch, famous hush puppies. Must-visit Crystal Coast landmark.
- **Captain Bill's Waterfront Restaurant** — On the Bogue Sound waterfront. Known for fresh seafood, steamed oysters, local atmosphere. Boat-accessible.
- **Ruddy Duck Tavern** — Casual waterfront spot with live music. Good for groups after fishing trips.
- **El's Drive-In** — Local institution since 1959. Famous shrimp burgers, not waterfront but pure Morehead City culture.

## Beaufort Historic Waterfront
- **Aqua Restaurant** — Upscale dining with waterfront views. Fresh local seafood, craft cocktails. Special occasion dining.
- **Clawson's 1905 Restaurant** — Historic building, casual fine dining. Local seafood specialties.
- **Beaufort Grocery Co** — Creative menu using local ingredients. Wine selection. Popular with locals.
- **The Spot** — Casual waterfront bar and grill. Good for lunch after fishing Beaufort Inlet.

## Atlantic Beach/Emerald Isle
- **White Swan Bar-B-Q** — Not waterfront but legendary eastern NC barbecue. Worth the trip inland.
- **The Oceanana Family Restaurant** — Beachfront dining, family-friendly. Classic beach food.
- **El Zarape** — Mexican food in Atlantic Beach. Popular with fishing crews.

## Dock-and-Dine Accessible
- **Sanitary Fish Market** — Downtown Morehead City waterfront, dinghy access
- **Captain Bill's** — Bogue Sound waterfront, direct boat access
- **Beaufort waterfront restaurants** — Many accessible by dinghy from anchorage

## Local Specialties
- **Fresh Local Seafood** — Flounder, drum, shrimp, oysters, blue crab
- **Eastern NC Barbecue** — Whole hog, vinegar-based sauce (different from western NC)
- **Hush Puppies** — Sanitary Fish Market's are legendary
- **She-crab Soup** — Regional specialty
- **Calabash Shrimp** — Lightly battered and fried

## Seasonal Notes
- **Summer** — Peak season, reservations recommended
- **Fall/Winter** — Many seasonal places close or reduce hours
- **Crab Season** — Blue crab peaks in summer/early fall
- **Oyster Season** — Traditional R months (September-April)

## Port32 Tie-ins
- Downtown Morehead City puts you walking distance to waterfront dining
- Historic district atmosphere — Carolina coastal culture
- Easy access to both Morehead City and Beaufort dining scenes
- Provisions and ice available downtown for boat trips

## Card Types
- **Waterfront dining guide** — best spots by boat or walking from marina
- **Local seafood spotlight** — seasonal specialties and where to find them
- **Historic Beaufort dining** — exploring the colonial waterfront town
- **Post-fishing feast** — where to celebrate a good day on the water
- **Provisions guide** — stocking up in historic downtown Morehead City
