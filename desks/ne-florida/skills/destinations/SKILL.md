# Destinations — NE Florida

Write boating destination and day trip cards for First Coast captains.

## River Destinations

### St. Johns River North
- **Green Cove Springs** — Historic river town, clay cliffs, spring-fed water. 45 min upriver, good restaurants
- **Doctors Lake** — Protected cove off main river. Swimming, picnicking, family destination
- **Black Creek** — Tributary exploration, scenic, shallow draft needed
- **Shands Bridge** — I-295 bridge area, good fishing, boat ramp access

### St. Johns River South  
- **Mandarin** — Upscale river community, Clark's Fish Camp, historic area
- **Julington Creek** — Protected waters, residential area, good for kids
- **Orange Park** — River access to suburban amenities, restaurants
- **Palatka run** — Full day trip, historic downtown, deeper into "real" Florida

## Coastal/Island Destinations

### Amelia Island
- **Fernandina Beach Harbor** — Historic shrimp village, restaurants, shops, marina facilities
- **Amelia Island State Park** — South end, pristine beaches, horseback riding on beach
- **Fort Clinch State Park** — Civil War fort, fishing pier, nature trails
- **Main Beach** — Town center area, walkable to shops and restaurants

### Nassau Sound Area
- **Tiger Island** — Shallow water destination, pristine beaches, no facilities
- **Rays** — Follow the rays in spring, cobia fishing, scenic run
- **Nassau Sound flats** — Low tide only, redfish watching, nature photography
- **Bell River** — Tributary exploration, manatees in winter

### North of Jacksonville
- **Cumberland Island, GA** — Wild horses, ruins, national seashore (check crossing regulations)
- **Kings Bay** — ICW destination, submarine base area (respect restricted zones)
- **St. Marys, GA** — Historic waterfront town, good restaurants, frontier heritage

### South of Jacksonville
- **St. Augustine** — Oldest city, historic architecture, excellent restaurants
- **Matanzas Inlet** — Scenic inlet, good fishing, less crowded than Mayport
- **Washington Oaks Gardens** — State park with unique coquina rock beach formations

## Day Trip Combinations

### River + Ocean
- **Morning river fishing** → **Afternoon Amelia Island beach**
- **Sunrise at Mayport jetties** → **Lunch in Fernandina** → **Afternoon river run**
- **Green Cove Springs breakfast** → **Offshore fishing** → **Atlantic Beach dinner**

### Historical Tours
- **Fort Clinch** → **Fernandina Historic District** → **Sunset at jetties**
- **Kingsley Plantation** → **Fort George River** → **Mayport village**
- **St. Augustine Castillo** → **ICW scenic run** → **Return via ocean**

### Family Adventures
- **Amelia Island State Park** (horses and beach) → **Ice cream in Fernandina**
- **Jacksonville Zoo** (by river) → **Picnic at Sisters Creek**
- **Huguenot Memorial Park** → **Beach camping overnight**

## Seasonal Considerations
- **Winter** — river destinations more comfortable, fewer crowds
- **Spring** — perfect weather for long runs, wildflowers at parks
- **Summer** — beach destinations peak, afternoon thunderstorms
- **Fall** — excellent weather, fishing and sightseeing combinations

## Port32 Advantages
- **Central location** — equal distance to river and ocean destinations
- **Fuel dock** — top off for longer runs to St. Augustine or Georgia
- **Valet service** — leave early, return late, boat handled
- **River access** — immediate access to St. Johns, no inlet to cross

## Navigation Notes
- **ICW markers** — well marked but pay attention, shoaling common
- **Georgia waters** — different regulations, check requirements
- **Military zones** — respect all restricted areas, especially around Kings Bay
- **Tidal planning** — low tide makes some destinations inaccessible
