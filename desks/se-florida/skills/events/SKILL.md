# Events — Southeast Florida

Write event and tournament cards for SE Florida boating community.

## Major Annual Events

### Fort Lauderdale International Boat Show (October/November)
- **Location**: Multiple venues throughout Fort Lauderdale
- **Duration**: 5 days, typically late October/early November
- **Scope**: World's largest boat show — yachts, sportfish, center consoles
- **Port32 connection**: Fort Lauderdale marina perfect base for show attendance
- **Parking/transport**: Water taxi and marina shuttle services during show
- **Industry focus**: Major yacht and sportfish manufacturers, new model debuts

### Palm Beach International Boat Show (March)
- **Location**: West Palm Beach Convention Center and waterfront
- **Duration**: 4 days in March
- **Focus**: Luxury yachts, sportfishing boats, marine accessories
- **Port32 connection**: Palm Beach Gardens marina 30 minutes south
- **Features**: In-water displays, land exhibits, fishing seminars

### Miami International Boat Show (February) 
- **Location**: Miami Beach Convention Center and marinas
- **Duration**: 4 days in February
- **Scope**: Largest boat show in the US by attendance
- **Access**: 45 minutes south from Fort Lauderdale marina by boat
- **Focus**: All categories — fishing, yachting, watersports

## Fishing Tournaments

### Sailfish Tournament Season (December-April)

#### Fort Lauderdale Sailfish Tournament
- **When**: January (specific dates vary)
- **Base**: Multiple Fort Lauderdale marinas including Port32
- **Format**: Release tournament, team competition
- **Entry**: Professional and amateur divisions
- **Weigh-in**: Evening festivities and awards

#### Lighthouse Point Sailfish Tournament  
- **When**: Peak sailfish season (Dec-Mar dates vary)
- **Base**: Port32 Lighthouse Point — heart of Sailfish Alley
- **Local advantage**: Closest marina to prime sailfish grounds
- **Format**: Release tournament with daily and overall prizes

#### Palm Beach Sailfish Derby
- **When**: Multiple dates during season
- **Base**: Palm Beach area marinas
- **Access**: 30 minutes from Port32 Palm Beach Gardens
- **Prize structure**: Daily and overall awards

### Summer Tournaments (May-August)

#### Mahi-Mahi Tournaments
- **Season**: Peak mahi season when Gulf Stream is close
- **Format**: Weight tournaments, single-day events
- **Locations**: Various SE Florida ports
- **Strategy**: Early morning Gulf Stream runs

#### Mixed Bag Tournaments
- **Format**: Multiple species, points-based scoring
- **Season**: Year-round, but peak summer
- **Categories**: Offshore and nearshore divisions

### King Mackerel Tournament Circuit (October-March)
- **Season**: King mackerel peak season
- **Venues**: Rotate between SE Florida ports
- **Format**: Weight tournaments, team fishing
- **Techniques**: Slow trolling, downrigger fishing

## Seasonal Event Planning

### Winter Season (December-April)
- **Peak tournament season**: Sailfish and king mackerel events
- **Boat show season**: Major shows require advance marina reservations
- **Yacht season**: Charter and brokerage activity peaks
- **Weather advantage**: Best conditions for events and tournaments

### Summer Season (May-September)
- **Offshore tournaments**: Mahi, wahoo, and mixed bag events
- **Afternoon storm considerations**: Morning start times typical
- **Local events**: Community festivals and social events
- **Hurricane season planning**: Event cancellation policies

### Fall Transition (October-November)
- **Fort Lauderdale Boat Show**: Biggest event of the year
- **Tournament season transition**: Summer offshore to winter sailfish
- **Hurricane season end**: Event scheduling resumes
